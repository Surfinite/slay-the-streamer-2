**Small new things I noticed in v3 specifically:**

- **The escalation receipt routing mechanism is left ambiguous** (`§"30-failure escalation receipt"`): "exposes an event `EscalationRequested` (or just calls `ModEntry.SendEscalationReceipt()` via a static facade)." Pin the event-based version; static facade couples YouTubeChatService to ModEntry's surface and undermines TI extractability. Trivial decision, just nail it.

- **MultiChatService constructor's lambda subscriptions are never unsubscribed.** `child.ConnectionStateChanged += (s, e) => OnChildConnectionStateChanged(name, s, e);` captures `name` but there's no matching `-=` in Dispose. If a child is ever shared across multiple aggregators (won't happen in v1, but the TI-extraction story suggests futures where it might), the closure keeps the multi alive past Dispose. Spec-level cleanliness; either unsubscribe in Dispose or document the ownership assumption.

- **`_consecutive429Count` reset condition is slightly ambiguous.** "Reset on next 2xx" — but a 2xx `get_live_chat` response with no continuation also goes to Reconnecting (broadcast-ended). Should that reset the 429 counter? Probably yes (the rate-limit pressure is over) but the spec's English is loose. Pin "reset on any 2xx response, not just on ConnectedReadOnly transition."

- **`IsMultiPlatformConfigured` derivation contradiction.** Spec text says VoteCoordinator "derives" it from the MultiChatService; ModEntry code passes it explicitly as a constructor param. Pick one — explicit-param is cleaner (the coordinator doesn't need to know about MultiChatService's internals).

- **TI extraction modularity section slightly oversells "strippability."** Deleting `Ti/Chat/YouTubeChat/` leaves `ChatPlatformNames.YouTube` orphaned, `VoteSession.PlatformOf` checking a never-encountered prefix, and `VoteTallyLabel.PlatformDisplayOrder` with a dead entry. All harmless at runtime; none clean. A future porter doing the extraction will want a quick cleanup pass. Either move `ChatPlatformNames.YouTube` into the YouTubeChat folder (as the section already mentions as an option — just commit) or add a one-line "post-strip cleanup" item to the section.

- **`Vote [42]:` syntax in receipts** — minor UX concern. Most chat viewers won't know what the bracketed number means; the streamer needs to explain or it just looks like a glitch. Spec mentions verbal encouragement for `!NN` syntax but not for `[NN]` itself. Consider one-time receipt at vote-mode start that explains: `Tip: append "!42" to vote in vote 42 specifically (helps stream-delayed viewers).`

- **Initial-poll backlog suppression drops some genuinely-new messages.** Spec acknowledges this. Worth flagging in Failure modes table — magnitude is ~1–3% of votes depending on page-load-to-first-poll gap. Operator validation could measure.

None of those are blockers. The C5 drop (no startup discoverability receipt) plus the `[42]:` ambiguity together mean YT viewers still have a slightly worse onboarding than Twitch viewers, but that's acceptable v1 territory and the streamer compensates verbally.

**Recommendation**: go to `writing-plans`. The remaining items are implementation-time decisions or operator-validation measurements, not design-time blockers. The spec has earned its way to plan generation.
